What the Community of Madrid is promoting in AI

The regional government has articulated several complementary initiatives. These are not slogans: they have budgets, deadlines, and real use cases. These are the most relevant for a Madrid SME:

AI Strategy (2030)

Regional plan to position Madrid as a European reference in artificial intelligence: laboratory, advanced factory, and space for SMEs and startups to test projects, according to Telemadrid.

LADIA

Draft Law on Digital Administration and Artificial Intelligence: ethical, transparent, and supervised use of AI in public services, aligned with European regulations (public consultation).

AI Center of Excellence

Infrastructure in Digitaliza Madrid: sovereign cloud, advanced computing, and automation of repetitive tasks in administration. Nearly 200 use cases identified, 102 already operational (Europa Press).

EDIH Madrid Region 2.0

European digital innovation hub: technology testing, training, consulting, and AI Helpdesk for SMEs. It has supported more than 550 companies (EDIH Madrid Region).

In parallel, the Community has promoted professional training in AI and Big Data and strengthens the demand for technical talent. The message is clear: Madrid bets on AI as regional infrastructure, not as a passing trend.

What this means for your SME in Madrid

The institutional commitment creates opportunity but also competitive pressure. Three direct effects:

  1. Your clients and suppliers expect more digitization. If the administration automates procedures and assistants, an SME with manual processes in Excel loses agility compared to competitors who have already integrated ERP, CRM, and automations.
  2. There are resources to explore, but not to produce alone. EDIH and public programs help test technologies and train teams. Moving from testing to a production system — connected to Odoo, your website, or your CRM — requires development and integration.
  3. Compliance is no longer optional. LADIA and the European AI Regulation (AI Act) set the path: traceability, human oversight, data protection. A serious AI agency in Madrid should address this from the pilot, not at the end.

How to choose an AI agency in Madrid (quick checklist)

  • Start with a process, not the technology. "We want AI" is not a project; "automate the classification of support tickets" is.
  • Request a limited pilot with metrics. Hours saved, errors reduced, response time. Madrid bets on measurable use cases; your company should too.
  • Demand integration, not silos. An isolated chatbot or a PDF report does not transform operations. It must connect with what you already use.
  • Review data and compliance. Where they are stored, who accesses them, what happens if the model makes a mistake.
  • Combine public resources with private implementation. EDIH can help you explore; an agency like Megasoluciones takes you to production and maintenance.

Recommended first steps

  1. Identify the process that consumes the most time (reports, registrations, reconciliations, repetitive attention).
  2. Consult resources from the Madrid ecosystem (EDIH, FP training) if you are still in the exploratory phase.
  3. Define a 4–8 week pilot with concrete deliverables and a closed budget.
  4. Validate with real data before scaling to more departments.
  5. Document and monitor: logs, human reviews where necessary, evolution plan.
