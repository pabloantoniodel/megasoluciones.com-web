Spanish SMEs lose hours every week on tasks that a computer can do better: copying data between Excel and the ERP, generating reports, sending follow-up emails, or reconciling invoices. Process automation is not just for large corporations: today it is accessible, cost-effective, and can be implemented in weeks.

Which processes to automate first in an SME

Prioritize processes that meet these three conditions: they are often repeated, have clear rules, and consume time from qualified personnel.

  • Billing and collections: invoice generation, sending to the client, and payment reminders.
  • Data synchronization: between CRM, spreadsheet, and accounting software.
  • Reporting: weekly sales, stock, or KPI reports that are currently assembled manually.
  • Customer onboarding: account creation, folders, and welcome emails.

How to calculate the ROI of an automation

Multiply the weekly hours dedicated to the process by the employee's hourly cost. If a report takes 5 h/week at €25/h, that's €650/month. An automation costing €400/month that eliminates 80% of the work pays for itself in the first month.

RPA, APIs, or hybrid: what fits your SME

If your tools have an API (Odoo, Google Workspace, many CRMs), API automation is more stable and cheaper to maintain. If there is only a graphical interface, RPA may be the only option. In most Spanish SMEs, we recommend starting with a pilot of a single process.

Steps to implement automations

  1. 1-2 hour audit: map the current process.
  2. Prioritize by impact (hours × frequency × errors).
  3. Pilot in 3-4 weeks with a limited workflow.
  4. Measure results and scale to other departments.
